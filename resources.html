Patient Resources

Access clinical regulators, professional colleges, and directories to help make the best decisions for your health.

Our team at Prime Physiotherapy is here to make the best decisions for our patients. We want you to be informed about these decisions and be part of the conversation when it comes to the design of your treatment plan. We have gathered quick links to our governing colleges and regulators, which include dedicated public safety and patient information boards.

College of Physiotherapists of Ontario

The regulatory body that protects the public interest by ensuring registered physiotherapists in Ontario practice safely, ethically, and competently.

Visit Regulator Site →

Canadian Physiotherapy Association

Representing over 15,000 members, the CPA provides advocacy, professional development, and resources to advance the physiotherapy profession in Canada.

Visit CPA Site →

Canadian Academy of Manipulative Physiotherapy

CAMPT-certified physiotherapists (FCAMPT) have completed extensive post-graduate education in manual therapy, reaching international clinical standards.

Visit CAMPT Site →

Pelvic Health Solutions

An evidence-based training body for pelvic health, teaching internal and external techniques to target pelvic floor muscle dysfunction, incontinence, and pain.

Visit Pelvic Health →

College of Massage Therapists of Ontario

The regulatory body established by the Ontario government to regulate the practice of Registered Massage Therapy (RMT) and ensure public protection.

Visit CMTO Site →

Registered Massage Therapists' Association

RMTAO is the member-driven professional association representing RMTs in Ontario, advocating for high clinical standards and professional growth.

Visit RMTAO Site →

College of Kinesiologists of Ontario

CKO regulates the practice of kinesiology in Ontario, ensuring that Registered Kinesiologists provide safe, ethical, and effective exercise programs.

Visit CKO Site →

Ontario Kinesiology Association

Representing kinesiologists across Ontario, the OKA supports public awareness of the benefits of active kinesiology and movement therapy.

Visit OKA Site →
